Kesar, also known as saffron, holds significant economic importance for the Indian region of Jammu and Kashmir.

Trade: Saffron is one of the most expensive spices globally, making it a valuable commodity for trade. Jammu and Kashmir's saffron trade contributes significantly to the region's economy through both domestic and international sales. The export of high-quality saffron enhances India's trade balance and brings in foreign exchange earnings.

Tourism: The cultivation of saffron in Jammu and Kashmir attracts tourists, contributing to the region's tourism industry. Tourists visit saffron fields during the blooming season, boosting local businesses such as hotels, restaurants, and handicraft shops. Saffron-based products, such as sweets and cosmetics, also attract tourists, further stimulating economic activity in the region.

Transportation: The saffron industry necessitates a well-developed transportation infrastructure to facilitate the timely transportation of saffron from farms to markets. This requirement creates employment opportunities in transportation and logistics sectors, supporting the livelihoods of many individuals involved in the transportation chain.

Agricultural Growth: Saffron cultivation promotes agricultural growth in Jammu and Kashmir. It encourages farmers to adopt modern agricultural practices and invest in irrigation systems, leading to increased productivity and income levels. Moreover, the cultivation of saffron contributes to soil conservation and biodiversity preservation in the region, enhancing the overall sustainability of agriculture.

Overall, saffron cultivation plays a vital role in the economic development of Jammu and Kashmir, contributing to trade, tourism, transportation, and agricultural growth, while also preserving the cultural heritage of the region.

English-----set of instructions 

here are the instructions to make Phirni with Kesar Milkshake:

Ingredients:

1 cup rice
1 liter milk
1 cup sugar
A few strands of saffron (kesar)
1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews)
1 teaspoon cardamom powder
Rose water (optional)
Ice cubes (for serving)
For Kesar Milkshake:

Heat 1 cup of milk in a saucepan until it comes to a simmer.
Add a few strands of saffron (kesar) to the hot milk and let it steep for 10-15 minutes to infuse the flavor and color.
Once infused, let the saffron milk cool down to room temperature.
In a blender, combine the saffron milk with the remaining milk, sugar, and a pinch of cardamom powder.
Blend until smooth and frothy.
Transfer the Kesar Milkshake to serving glasses and refrigerate until ready to serve.
For Phirni:

Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.
After soaking, drain the water from the rice and grind it into a coarse paste using a blender or food processor.
In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the remaining milk to a boil.
Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add the ground rice paste, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
Cook the mixture on low heat, stirring frequently, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens to a creamy consistency (about 20-25 minutes).
Add sugar and continue to cook for another 5-10 minutes until the sugar is completely dissolved and the Phirni reaches the desired sweetness.
Remove the pan from heat and stir in the chopped nuts, remaining cardamom powder, and a splash of rose water (if using).
Let the Phirni cool down to room temperature.
Once cooled, transfer the Phirni to serving bowls or glasses.
Refrigerate the Phirni for at least 2-3 hours before serving to chill and set.
Serve the chilled Phirni topped with a drizzle of Kesar Milkshake and garnished with additional chopped nuts and saffron strands.
Optionally, serve with ice cubes on the side for an extra refreshing touch.
